The Devil’s Advocate

Gear up as you play the devil’s advocate and go neck and neck against the contenders from other teams, debating against and contradicting not just your opposition but even yourself?! Feel the rush as you speak in favour of as well as against the topic provided to you on-the-spot while being critiqued on your content, confidence and spontaneity. There’s no room for error as you attempt to sweep through the competition and clinch victory. So, will you rise to the occasion or fall prey to the heat of the moment and fumble? Only one way to find out.

Number of rounds: 2

Event for individual participants: maximum 3 from each school

Regarding rounds

Number of rounds: 2 There will be 2 rounds in which participants are to speak both for and against the topic allotted. Only half of those competing will qualify for the second (and final round). In the second round, participants need to only speak for or against their topics. The remaining participants are permitted to ask them questions based on their speech and this will be considered for marking.

Rules and regulations

Topics will be given by the hosts. Once allotted, the topics are final and participants cannot request for different topics. Participants are expected to avoid exceeding the time allotted for preparation and cause unnecessary delays/disruptions. Signal to switch motions will be provided by anchors. Participants will speak for 4-5 minutes. Cameras must be turned on during time of presentation. Reading the speech from a page or the screen must be avoided. Time limit and judging criteria Time limit: 2 minutes

For round 1:

1.Content (3)

2.Organisation and flow – structural format used [introduction, conclusion] (2)

3.Fluency (2)

4.Vocabulary (2)

5.Body language (1)

For round 2:

1.Content (3) 2.Organisation and flow – structural format used [introduction, conclusion] (2) 3.Fluency and vocabulary (3) 4.Responses to questions from other participants(2)
